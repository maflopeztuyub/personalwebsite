About Me

My Story

I grew up in Molas, a small community outside of Merida, Yucatan, where I developed a deep passion for mathematics from an early age. That passion led me to represent Mexico in international math competitions starting at age 11, and eventually to pursue Computer Engineering at Georgia Tech.

Growing up in a community where access to educational resources was limited shaped my commitment to giving back through tutoring, coaching, and outreach.

At Georgia Tech, I am part of the AR/VR Haptics subteam within the Vertically Integrated Projects program, where I develop ESP32-based firmware and BLE communication systems for haptic feedback hardware. I also participate in hackathons and continue to contribute to math and informatics olympiad communities in Mexico.

Featured Projects

HypeHub

A real-time AI-powered dance coaching platform built at Hacklytics 2026. HypeHub uses MediaPipe Pose for computer vision-based body landmark detection and skeletal overlay, enabling frame-by-frame form analysis against reference choreography. The system delivers personalized audio feedback via ElevenLabs TTS and surfaces performance analytics using KMeans clustering. The full pipeline runs from webcam input to structured feedback in under 2 seconds.

Technologies Used:

MediaPipe Pose ElevenLabs TTS KMeans Clustering Gemini API Next.js

Your Contributions: Co-developed the skeletal overlay and form analysis pipeline, integrated ElevenLabs audio feedback, and engineered the AI content scheduling feature that generates optimized social media posts from session highlights.

ESP32 Haptic Feedback System

As part of Georgia Tech's Vertically Integrated Projects program, I am developing ESP32-based haptic feedback hardware for AR/VR headsets. The system integrates MPU6050 IMU sensors over I2C to capture 6-DOF motion data for real-time gesture recognition. A BLE communication layer streams sensor data and receives haptic commands wirelessly from a host device. Firmware is developed in C++ with Arduino and PlatformIO, with simulation and debugging in Wokwi prior to hardware deployment.

Technologies Used:

ESP32 C++ / Arduino PlatformIO BLE MPU6050 IMU Wokwi

Your Contributions: Implementing the BLE firmware layer, I2C sensor integration, and a simulation-based debugging workflow to reduce hardware iteration cycles before deployment.
